Glycolysis

Glycolysis (Emdben-Meyerhof-Parnas Pathway) is a biochemical pathway that breaks down 1 molecule of glucose through a series of reactions, giving energy and 2 molecules of pyruvate. Pyruvate is a 'Jack-of-all-trade' as you might call it as it is a main intermediate in a lot of reactions like Gluconeogenesis, Glycogenolysis, Citric Acid Cycle to mention a few

Urea Cycle

This is a biomedical pathway that 'reforms' the toxic ammonia into water soluble urea that can be easily filtered out of the blood by the kidneys in form of urine. The Urea Cycle is also called the Ornithine Pathway or the Krebs-Henseleit Pathway

Citric Acid Cycle

This is the biochemical pathway in which pyruvate goes through a series of reactions to produce reducing equivalents that produce energy (in form of ATP) in the ETC (Electron Transport Chain). It is also called the Kreb's cycle or the Tricarboxylic Acid (TCA)
